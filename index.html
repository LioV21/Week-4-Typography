NVIDIA

History

NVIDIA was founded in 1993 by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem. Initially, the company focused on the gaming market, where it established itself by developing high-performance GPUs. Over the years, NVIDIA has diversified its product offerings to include not just gaming GPUs but also GPUs for professional visualization, data center, and automotive markets. It introduced the GeForce series in 1999, which became popular in gaming and multimedia industries.

Products

NVIDIA is known for its GPUs, which are used in a variety of industries including gaming, professional visualization, and artificial intelligence. Its product lineup includes the GeForce series for gamers, the Quadro series for professionals, and the Tesla series designed for high-performance computing. In recent years, NVIDIA has also ventured into autonomous vehicles and robotics, leveraging its expertise in artificial intelligence and deep learning.

Acquisitions

Over the years, NVIDIA has made several strategic acquisitions to expand its portfolio. In 2006, it acquired the company PortalPlayer, which was instrumental in developing the first-generation iPod. In 2011, NVIDIA acquired Icera, a baseband chipmaker, to expand its presence in the mobile market. The company continued to make key acquisitions, including Mellanox in 2020 and ARM in a highly-publicized deal.
